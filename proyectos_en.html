CO₂ Sensor

The CO₂ Sensor project, developed by 3rd year ESO students at I.E.S. Kursaal in Algeciras, aims to monitor air quality in classrooms by measuring carbon dioxide (CO₂) levels. This initiative was particularly important during the COVID-19 pandemic to ensure proper ventilation in closed spaces.

The system is based on the MH-Z19B sensor, which uses non-dispersive infrared (NDIR) technology to measure CO₂ levels. The ESP8266 microcontroller (NodeMCU) is used for data processing and transmission. Additional sensors, such as DHT11/DHT22, measure temperature and humidity, while the OLED display SSD1306 provides real-time data visualization. The collected data is transmitted to the ThingSpeak platform for remote monitoring and analysis.

Technologies used:

  • MH-Z19B Sensor (NDIR)
  • ESP8266 Microcontroller (NodeMCU)
  • DHT11/DHT22 Sensors
  • OLED Display SSD1306
  • ThingSpeak Platform

Key features:

  • Real-time monitoring of CO₂, temperature, and humidity
  • Automatic data transmission to ThingSpeak
  • Graph visualization
  • ThingSpeak Channel: 1335280

SmartDoor

The Smart Door project, developed by 4th year ESO students at IES Kursaal in Algeciras, focuses on designing an energy-efficient housing adapted to the extreme conditions of Antarctica. The initiative aims to optimize resource consumption, especially in heating, and facilitate the work of researchers in this environment.

One of the main innovations of the project is an entry system that minimizes heat loss by avoiding direct contact with the exterior. This results in a significant reduction in generator usage, which, in addition to saving fuel, helps reduce environmental pollution associated with supply transportation by ship.

The project is interdisciplinary, covering various areas of knowledge. In geology and history, materials used in Antarctic shelter construction and their historical context were investigated. From physics, the thermal behavior and structural resistance of a spherical housing were analyzed. In technology, electronic components were incorporated to automate the entry system. Architecture and materials engineering focused on the structural design of the base, while biology provided information on human needs and the use of plant materials in construction.

Research Areas:

  • Energy Efficiency
  • Architectural Design
  • Thermal Physics
  • Automation
  • Materials Engineering
  • Environmental Sustainability

Kursaatélite

The Kursaatélite project, developed by students at IES Kursaal in Algeciras, focuses on the design, construction, and testing of a nanosatellite prototype. This initiative is part of the educational project "Educación y Espacio," which promotes STEM vocations through real-space technology projects.

Kursaatélite is a small satellite designed to test various technologies and conduct scientific experiments in space. Its primary objectives include evaluating the performance of commercial electronic components in the space environment, testing a new attitude control system using reaction wheels, and demonstrating the feasibility of using 3D printing for satellite component manufacturing.

The project provides students with hands-on experience in satellite development, from the initial design and simulation phases to the construction, integration, and testing of the final prototype. This includes training in areas such as electronics, programming, mechanical design, and project management.

Research Areas:

  • Nanosatellite Design and Construction
  • Space Technology
  • STEM Education
  • Satellite Testing and Validation
  • 3D Printing in Aerospace
  • Attitude Control Systems

DustNet

The DustNet project, developed by students at IES Kursaal in Algeciras, aims to create a low-cost, open-source device to measure and analyze dust particles in the air. This project is particularly relevant in the context of increasing concern about air quality and its impact on health and the environment.

DustNet devices are designed to be easily assembled and calibrated by users, making them accessible for educational purposes, citizen science, and community monitoring initiatives. The project includes a comprehensive guide on building and using the device, as well as a platform for users to share their data and findings.

Research Areas:

  • Air Quality Monitoring
  • Citizen Science
  • Open Source Hardware
  • Environmental Data Analysis
  • Community Health
  • STEM Education
